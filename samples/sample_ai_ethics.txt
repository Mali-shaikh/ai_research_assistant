# Title: The Ethical Implications of Autonomous Decision-Making in Undergraduate AI Systems

## Abstract
As undergraduate students increasingly integrate Artificial Intelligence (AI) into their final-year projects, the ethical dimension of autonomous decision-making remains underexplored. This paper investigates the moral agency of students when designing AI systems that make decisions without human intervention. We propose a framework for ethical oversight in student-led AI development, focusing on accountability, transparency, and data privacy.

## Introduction
The rapid democratization of AI tools, such as Large Language Models (LLMs) and automated data processors, has revolutionized academic research. However, with this power comes significant responsibility. Many students prioritize performance over ethics during the development phase. This study aims to bridge the gap between technical implementation and ethical considerations.

## Methodology
We conducted a survey of 100 students from engineering and computer science backgrounds. Each student was asked to present their AI project's architecture and identify potential ethical "blind spots." Additionally, a qualitative analysis of 10 AI-based research projects was performed to evaluate the inclusion of ethics-by-design.

## Results
- 75% of projects lacked a dedicated section for ethical considerations.
- 55% of students were unaware of GDPR compliance requirements.
- 30% of researchers identified potential biases in their training datasets but did not implement mitigation strategies.

## Discussion
The lack of standardized ethical guidelines for student-scale AI projects is a significant concern. While professional AI researchers have institutional review boards (IRBs), undergraduate projects often fly under the radar. This can lead to the replication of historical biases in data.

## Conclusion
Ethical literacy must be a core component of research methodologies. We recommend that all final-year projects involving AI include an 'Ethics Impact Statement' to ensure that the next generation of researchers is well-versed in responsible AI development.
